## Why Recruiting Needs an AI Agent

Recruiting in 2026 is underwater. The gap between the number of applications per role and the number of hours a recruiter has to review them has become unbridgeable by human effort alone.

- Volume is exploding. AI-assisted applications mean a single opening can attract thousands of resumes overnight. Most never get read.
- Keyword filters destroy signal. Legacy applicant tracking systems rely on keyword matching that rejects strong candidates and promotes keyword-stuffed resumes.
- Phone screens are a time sink. Each twenty-minute screen costs thirty or more minutes of recruiter time once scheduling, prep, and notes are included. Multiply by thirty candidates and a week is gone.
- Verification happens too late. Resume claims are rarely checked until the offer stage, wasting entire pipelines on candidates who exaggerated their experience.

Renate exists because this job should not take a team of humans weeks to do. It should take one AI agent hours.

## How Renate Works

You submit a job. Renate takes it from there. Step by step:

### 1. You fill out one form

The recruiter logs in and completes a single job requisition. This is the only manual input Renate needs. The form captures everything: role details, must-have and nice-to-have skills, seniority expectations, location and work model, compensation range, and any custom questions you want asked during the voice screen.

You can set scoring weights, telling Renate that skills matter more than credentials, or that project evidence matters more than years of experience. Once you click submit, the form becomes a structured Job Profile: the single source of truth Renate uses for every decision it makes.

### 2. Renate goes hunting

The moment a Job Profile exists, Renate starts actively sourcing. It searches professional networks, resume databases, developer communities, and other integrated talent pools, translating your requirements into targeted queries and reaching out to high-fit candidates with personalized outreach.

Two streams of talent flow into every role at the same time: inbound applicants who found the posting, and sourced candidates Renate discovered on its own. The recruiter never touches LinkedIn and never writes a cold message. Renate handles all of it.

### 3. Every resume parsed and understood

Every application, whether sourced or inbound, gets ingested and parsed into a normalized candidate profile. PDFs, Word documents, even images of resumes are all converted into a structured schema covering work history, education, skills, projects, certifications, and links. Every candidate enters the evaluation pipeline on equal footing, regardless of source or format.

### 4. The four-stage scoring pipeline

Every resume passes through four sequential stages, each more precise than the last.

- Stage one is parsing and normalization. Renate extracts structured data from raw resumes, normalizes titles, skills, and dates into a common taxonomy, and flags suspicious or malformed submissions.
- Stage two is hard-requirement gating. Renate checks non-negotiable requirements such as minimum years, required certifications, and work authorization, and eliminates clearly unqualified candidates early.
- Stage three is semantic fit scoring. An LLM evaluates each surviving candidate against the Job Profile across multiple dimensions: skills, experience relevance, project impact, domain knowledge, and career trajectory. It produces a weighted score from zero to one hundred with per-dimension breakdowns.
- Stage four is ranking and top fifty selection. All scored candidates are ranked, the top fifty advance, and a calibration pass ensures fairness across processing batches.

At the end of this pipeline, Renate has a ranked list. The top fifty candidates advance. A calibration pass ensures scores remain fair regardless of when a resume was processed or how strong a particular batch was.

### 5. The top ten get a phone call

From the top fifty, Renate selects the ten strongest candidates using a tightened rubric that prioritizes real project evidence over generic skill claims. Each candidate receives an automated email inviting them to book a voice call with Renate, or take it on demand.

### 6. Renate interviews by voice

Renate conducts a real, adaptive voice interview with each of the top ten, a fifteen to twenty-five minute conversation that feels natural, not scripted. Before each call, Renate already knows the candidate inside out: their score, strengths and weaknesses, resume gaps, and the custom questions you want answered.

During the call, Renate builds rapport and explains the process naturally; asks role-specific questions tied directly to the Job Profile; verifies resume claims with targeted follow-ups, catching exaggeration before it wastes your time; probes weak areas to separate real blockers from surface-level omissions; captures what no resume can, including motivation, communication style, problem-solving approach, availability, and compensation expectations; and adapts in real time based on candidate answers, exactly like a skilled human screener.

### 7. Post-call intelligence

The moment a call ends, Renate processes the transcript and produces a full intelligence package for each candidate: a full speaker-labeled transcript of the conversation; an updated score, where the post-call score may confirm, raise, or lower the resume-stage score; a verification verdict on every major resume claim, marked confirmed, partially confirmed, unverified, or contradicted; behavioral signals covering clarity, confidence, depth of knowledge, and communication quality; and a clear recommendation: Strong Yes, Yes, Maybe, or No.

### 8. Your shortlist, zero guesswork

Renate delivers a final, evidence-backed shortlist of ten candidates. For each one you see the resume score and the post-call score side by side, a skill and gap visualization, verification status for every major claim, the full call transcript and AI summary, and Renate's recommendation. Your next action is one click: Invite.

### 9. One click to schedule

When you click Invite, Renate generates a scheduling link, sends personalized emails to both you and the candidate, coordinates calendars, creates the video conference room, and adds the event with reminders. No back-and-forth. Your only job is to show up.

### 10. Renate sits in and takes notes

When the live interview starts, Renate joins as a silent notetaker. It records and transcribes in real time, identifies speakers, and tracks every question and answer. When the meeting ends, you receive the full transcript, an executive summary, key highlights and action items, and a comparison against the earlier call and resume signals, flagging any consistency issues. You never replay a recording. You never scramble for notes. You just decide.
